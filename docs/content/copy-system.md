# AUVORA Copy System

## Voice Intent
AUVORA copy should feel editorial, precise, and premium. It should guide decisions, not narrate endlessly.

## Tone Rules
- Calm confidence over hype.
- Specific over poetic vagueness.
- Decisive over suggestive.
- Warm restraint over performative luxury language.

## Core Writing Pattern
- Lead with decision.
- Follow with one reason.
- End with one action (if needed).

Example:
- "Reach for the cleaner profile tonight. It keeps presence sharp without over-projecting."

## CTA Patterns
Use short, direct verbs.

Preferred:
- `Generate aura`
- `Refine output`
- `Save to history`
- `Start routine`
- `Log wear`
- `Use this direction`

Avoid:
- `Let’s go!`
- `Unlock magic`
- `Discover your vibe now`

## Placeholder Rules
- Show intent and format in one line.
- Avoid fake-personalized placeholders.
- Keep under 90 characters when possible.

Preferred placeholders:
- Aura input: "Describe the moment you’re stepping into."
- Scent situation: "Dinner after work, cleaner and more deliberate."
- Wardrobe import: "Brand - Fragrance name (one per line)"

## Empty-State Rules
Each empty state must include:
- What is missing
- Why it matters
- One next step

Template:
- "No wardrobe saved yet. Recommendations are strongest when tied to what you own. Add a few bottles to begin."

## Language To Avoid
- "Based on your preferences"
- "You might like"
- "Great question"
- "Main character"
- "Boss energy"
- "Fragrance queen"
- "Screams"
- Any influencer-style hype phrasing

## Microcopy Standards
- Labels: 1-3 words.
- Helper text: max 1 sentence.
- Error text: concrete, non-blaming, actionable.
- Success text: brief confirmation, no celebration spam.

## Premium Consistency Checks
- Can the sentence be cut by 30% without losing meaning?
- Does the line sound like a decision, not a suggestion list?
- Would this read well in dark mode at a glance?
